GTAJMAP

Type:

Robotics Command

Syntax:

value = GTAMAP

Description:

Returns a string showing which GTA indices represent joint positions (i.e., were set using GTAJ, or GTAE with an explicit point type of 1). Each character represents a nybble (half a byte, or 4 bits) in the form of a hexadecimal character (‘0’ to ‘F’), with the low GTAJ indices being stored in the high order bits, as illustrated below.

It is recommended to use GTAEMAP in preference to GTAJMAP, as it handles all point types in a uniform manner, so GTEMAP(1) is functionally equivalent to GTAJMAP, as a point type of 1 represents joint positions.

Examples:

GTA ( 0 ) = 1 , 2 , 3 , 4 , 5 , 6
GTA ( 1 ) = 1 , 2 , 3 , 4 , 5 , 6
GTAJ ( 2 ) = 1 , 2 , 3 , 4 , 5 , 6
GTA ( 3 ) = 1 , 2 , 3 , 4 , 5 , 6
GTAJ ( 4 ) = 1 , 2 , 3 , 4 , 5 , 6
GTA ( 5 ) = 1 , 2 , 3 , 4 , 5 , 6
GTA ( 6 ) = 1 , 2 , 3 , 4 , 5 , 6
GTAJ ( 7 ) = 1 , 2 , 3 , 4 , 5 , 6
GTAJ ( 8 ) = 1 , 2 , 3 , 4 , 5 , 6
GTAJ ( 9 ) = 1 , 2 , 3 , 4 , 5 , 6
GTA ( 10 ) = 1 , 2 , 3 , 4 , 5 , 6
GTAJ ( 11 ) = 1 , 2 , 3 , 4 , 5 , 6

PRINT "GTAJMAP = " ; LEFT ( GTAJMAP , 10 )
PRINT "GTAEMAP(0) = " ; LEFT ( GTAEMAP ( 0 ), 10 )
PRINT "GTAEMAP(1) = " ; LEFT ( GTAEMAP ( 1 ), 10 )
